GIS Portfolio

As the culminating assignment for Cartographic Design and Geovisualization, I was tasked with preparing a portfolio that showcases the cartographic products (maps, sketches, dashboards) I have designed in this course, as well as commenting on my process and improvements throughout. Here is my final design portfolio which reflects my coursework as well as incorporates feedback from peer design critiques.

Projects
brush Sketch Map of My Neighborhood
Description
This sketch map of my neighborhood a digital version of the original sketch map I drew by hand. This simplified map is primarily a reflection of my movements this past year in my hometown in Texas. I spent most of my time at home, at a park, or getting groceries. When creating this map, my intention was to place my home at the center and draw the paths I would take to the different locations I frequented. This map represents only a section of a larger region with a focus on three cities. I knew that the Rio Grande River and Gulf of Mexico would serve as distinct edges to the district, however, the edges North and West of my home are somewhat ambiguous.

Software Used: Google Jamboard
animation Foraging Behavior of Turkey vultures
Description
This is an animated map depicting the foraging behavior of turkey vultures Steamhouse 1 and Steamhouse 2. For this animation, I used the Turkey vulture data set. I ultimately decided to include the foraging behaviors of Steamhouse 1 and Steamhouse 2 given these two vultures followed similar paths unlike the other vultures. They were also observed during the same time period. In addition to the three variables pre-selected (individual.local.identifier, MODIS Land Terra Vegetation Indices, and Movebank Thermal Uplift), I enabled computedSpeed.

Software Used: DynamoVis
Data Source: Movebank Data Repository
mapMap Of Chicago Health Clinic, CTA Rail lines, and Median Household Income
Description
I chose to map Chicago Department of Public Health (CDPH) clinic locations and median household income by community area. Initially, I was curious about the number of clinic locations in Chicago and felt that it was appropriate to map this data in relation to median household income as the CDPH’s mission is to promote and improve health by implementing services that prioritize communities with the greatest need. I also included the CTA rail system to illustrate whether clinic locations were accessible by this mode of public transportation.

Software Used: QGIS-LTR, ColorBrewer
Data Sources: Chicago Data Portal, Chicago Health Atlas
dashboardHealth Factors by County Dashboard
Description
I chose to use the health-factors-county dataset and focused on the “medianhouseholdincome” and “FdInsPrc” variables. According to the US Covid Atlas, FdInsPrc is the percent of people without adequate food access (food insecurity). My homepage consists of a map displaying all the counties in the U.S. This map has a standard base map because its purpose as a reference map is to simply orient the users. Next, I wanted to add a choropleth map for each variable. I included both maps as sections under page 1, Map. Both choropleth maps were created with quantile classification and 5 bins. I used a different color scheme for both maps.

Software Used: Matico.app
Data Source: Matico.app built in dataset on health-factors by county in the U.S.
